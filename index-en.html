01 — About

About 簡介

Fang-Yi Huang is Associate Professor in the Department of Sociology at Soochow University, Taipei, where she also serves as Director of the Personnel Office and Executive Director of the AI and Society Research Center. She holds a Ph.D. in Sociology and Criminology & Law from the University of Florida, and previously held positions at Yuan Ze University and Academia Sinica.

She is Secretary-General of Research Committee 25 (Politics of Health Policy) at the International Political Science Association, and an Associate Editor of Health Politics.

02 — Research

Scholarship 學術

Her work asks one question: as labour markets shift and populations age, whom does social protection catch — and whom does it let fall through. Recent research centres on gender pension gaps across East Asia, Europe and the United States, and on old-age economic security for non-standard workers.

  • Medical Sociology
  • Social Welfare Legislation & Social Policy
  • Gender Studies
  • Older Women's Employment
  • Non-standard Employment
  • Old-Age Economic Security
  • AI and Social Welfare

An ongoing three-year NSTC project, How Pension Reform Reshapes Women's Late-Life Economic Security, uses dynamic simulation to examine female labour participation against pension adequacy (2025 — 2028).

03 — Publications

Publications 出版

Her articles appear in Journal of Asian Public Policy, PLOS ONE, Vaccine, Journal of Social Sciences and Philosophy, Social Policy & Social Work, and Taiwan Democracy Quarterly.

Recent work examines the blend of a Beveridgean approach with the Bismarckian model in Taiwan's pension system, and pension benefit levels for non-standard workers in Taiwan, Japan, and South Korea.
