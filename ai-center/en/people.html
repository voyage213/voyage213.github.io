People執行長與成員

Members are all Department of Sociology faculty who personally teach the AI course series and together sustain the Center’s teaching and research.

Fang-Yi Huang

Associate Professor, Dept. of Sociology, Soochow University; Executive Director, RCAIS ・ Fang-Yi Huang, Ph.D.

Her research spans medical sociology, social policy, gender studies, and old-age economic security, with a long-standing focus on how institutions leave marks across a lifetime — in health, work, and after retirement. In recent years she has brought AI into sociological teaching and research, offering "AI in Criminology" and "AI for Social Good," leading students from crime prediction and risk assessment to welfare technology, and reflecting on fairness and justice in the age of algorithms.

In practice, she uses microsimulation to analyze gender gaps in the pension systems of Taiwan, Japan, and Korea, translating her findings into the public-facing Taiwan–Japan–Korea Pension Calculator and the ten-minute documentary animation "Ten-Minute Countdown: Whose Old Age Does the System Remember?" — a concrete example of turning research into public communication. She also supervises several NSTC undergraduate research projects, including cross-national work on AI and gender.

Members

Members中心成員

All members are Department of Sociology faculty, spanning science-and-technology studies, sociological theory, gender studies, social policy, criminology, and social design. They personally teach the Center’s AI course series and together sustain its teaching and research.

3rd Director, 2025– DIRECTOR

Fang-Yi Huang

Associate Professor of Sociology; Executive Director, RCAIS

Current Executive Director. Teaches "AI in Criminology" and the cross-college course "AI for Social Good," and coordinates the Center’s lecture series, high-school AI summer camp, and international research. See the director profile above.

2nd Director, 2022–2024

Yu-Cheng Liu

Professor of Sociology

2nd Executive Director (2022–2024). Ph.D. in Sociology from NCCU; specializes in AI and society, ethnomethodology, systems theory, STS, and social/design thinking. He teaches "AI and Sociology" and focuses on "AI as a social phenomenon" and the social study of autonomous driving — among the first sociologists in Taiwan to study AI systematically. In 2026, leading an NSTC "chip-driven humanities" sub-project, his cross-disciplinary work After the Age of 80 (with Pao-Jui Kung of Shih Chien University and curator Hsiang-Wen Chen) won a Core77 Design Award in Speculative Design — the only Taiwanese team honored in the category this year.

Member

Chun-Mei Chang

Professor of Sociology

Specializes in sociological theory, STS, gender studies, and posthumanist thought, with long-standing work on the theory and translation of cyborg feminism. She teaches "AI and Cyborg Feminism" and "Ecological Wisdom and AI," leading students to think about human–machine relations, the body, and boundaries at the intersection of gender, ecology, and technology.

Member

Chien-Liang Chi

Lecturer, Dept. of Sociology

Teaches "AI-Assisted Social Design: How AI Changes Society," combining social-design methods with AI collaboration tools to help students understand AI’s impact on labor, education, and daily life, and to build design proposals that respond to real social problems.

1st Director, 2020–2021

Chung-Yang Yeh

Professor of Sociology

Specializes in social policy, welfare-state theory, pension systems, and comparative East Asian welfare regimes, focusing on how social-security systems respond to new risk structures amid digital transformation and population aging — a key research force for the Center’s "AI and social policy" agenda, and co-convener of the cross-departmental "Frontiers of AI and Welfare in East Asia" faculty community.
