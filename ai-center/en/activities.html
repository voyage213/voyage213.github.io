Cross-departmental faculty community ・ Human-Centered AI grant ・ AY 2025–26

East Asian Social Futures Lab: The Boundaries of AI and Welfare

Frontiers of AI and Welfare in East Asia

Generative AI and large language models are rapidly entering social-security and welfare decision-making — from eligibility determination and long-term-care assessment to fraud detection. Convened by Assoc. Prof. Fang-Yi Huang and Prof. Chung-Yang Yeh, with Prof. Hui-Fen Hung (Soochow Social Work) and Asst. Prof. Shao-Fen Li (Institute of Health and Welfare Policy, NYCU), the community opens dialogue around three tensions: algorithmic governance vs. distributive justice, protecting the vulnerable vs. human-centered ethics, and local data vs. the verifiability of cross-national comparison.

Shared lesson design
  • AI in Criminology: final projects used TSCS data to study AI early-warning bias in the social safety net; several groups extended these into theses
  • AI for Social Good: group debates on "the ethical red lines of algorithmic governance," with "My social-policy dialogue with AI" reflection journals
  • Social Statistics: five lectures ran in this slot; assignments moved to plain-language + AI-assisted practice
Five student learning outcomes
  • AI text analysis (vectorized semantic space)
  • Generative-AI literacy (rewrite risk, citation verification)
  • AI-assisted statistics (coding in plain language)
  • Immersive teaching (VR/AR long-term-care simulation)
  • Spotting algorithmic bias (sensitivity/specificity and accountability)
Policy output: draft white paper
  • Draft "White Paper on Social-Security Policy in the Age of AI," four chapters
  • Ch.1 model-adoption thresholds ・ Ch.2 bias-monitoring mechanisms
  • Ch.3 appeals and transparency ・ Ch.4 impact-assessment indicators
  • Focused on accessibility, fairness, and accountability for vulnerable groups; an e-edition will be published in AY 2026–27 and sent to relevant ministries

In planning ・ from 2027-07-06 ・ Soochow University, Room B608

High-school AI Summer Camp: "AI, Big Data & Immersive Creation" workshop (with a 2027 winter camp in planning)

Coordinated by Director Fang-Yi Huang, who also leads the "AI Criminology Lab." The two-day program gathers interdisciplinary speakers: Chien-Hua Tseng (AI and arts creation), Chung-Yang Yeh (Claude website design), Wei-Gong Liu (generative-AI works high schoolers can make), Yu-Cheng Liu (turning ideas into a storytelling exhibition), Kai-Yi Chin (AR/VR/MR story stage), an NVIDIA instructor (building deployable AI products), and Mei-Jung Chen (English songs and multimodal creation with generative AI). Five camp outputs: a podcast, an AI website, AI music, an AI picture book, and a short-video ad — leading high schoolers through hands-on AI creation from a sociological lens and extending the Center’s teaching to the high-school level.

In planning ・ Taiwanese Sociological Association Annual Meeting

Interdisciplinary forum: "AI Employability and Social Inequality"

As AI reshapes labor-market skill demands and the higher-education curriculum map, this forum makes "social inequality" the through-line, confronting who benefits and who is left behind. Speakers: Kai-Yi Chin (Dean, College of Big Data Management), Ming-Hsiang Su (Director, AI Applications Program), Ying-Hsiang Lin (Chinese Literature), and Mei-Jung Chen (English), moderated by Fang-Yi Huang (Sociology; Center Director) with ten questions across four themes.
