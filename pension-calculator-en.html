04 Formulas & notes 公式與說明

🇯🇵 Japan (dual-tier: Employees' Pension + National Pension)

  • Employees' Pension (monthly) = avg standard monthly wage × 5.481 ÷ 1000 × years insured
  • National Pension (monthly) = 795,000 ÷ 12 × (years ÷ 40); full ¥66,250/mo at 40 years (FY2023 / Reiwa 5)
  • Homemakers are "Category-3 insured": they receive the full National Pension without paying premiums.

🇹🇼 Taiwan (Labor Insurance and National Pension are mutually exclusive)

  • Labor Insurance old-age pension (≥15 yrs) = monthly insured wage × years × 1.55%; under 15 years only a lump sum is available.
  • National Pension = monthly insured amount × years × 0.65% + 3,772 (higher of this and × 1.3%).
  • Top insured-wage grade is 45,800; the 2023 National-Pension insured amount is 19,761.

🇰🇷 South Korea (dual-tier: National Pension + Basic Old-Age Pension, BOAP)

  • National Pension (monthly) = 1.275 × (A + B) × (years ÷ 20) ÷ 12; A = all-insured average, B = own average insured income. The (years ÷ 20) factor captures 50% at 10 years, +5% per additional year, and the post-20-year bonus (no monthly pension under 10 years).
  • Basic Pension (BOAP): full ₩323,180 (single). If the National Pension exceeds 150% of the base (₩484,770), a linked reduction applies — 323,180 − ⅔ × A-benefit — with the ₩161,590 supplementary pension as a floor; no benefit if recognized income exceeds the ₩2,020,000 selection threshold.
How to read this. This calculator is for institutional comparison and teaching. It uses 2023 statutory formulas and national average wages — an "ideal-type / current-institution" estimate that excludes wage growth, inflation, discounting and DC individual accounts (e.g. Taiwan's new Labor Pension), and does not equal any country's actual disbursements. It is not a guarantee or advice about any individual's pension. For actual benefits, consult each country's pension authority (Bureau of Labor Insurance, Japan Pension Service, National Pension Service of Korea).
